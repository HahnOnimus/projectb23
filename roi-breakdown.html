ROI Breakdown

Introduction

This document provides a detailed Return on Investment (ROI) analysis and projections for future equity holders of Project B23, based on market research. The analysis calculates potential monthly and annual ROI for equity holders who made a pioneer purchase at ₦2,000,000 ($1,333.33), ₦4,000,000 ($2,666.67), and ₦5,000,000 ($3,333.33), assuming different Monthly Active User (MAU) scenarios and subscription revenue models. All calculations use an exchange rate of ₦1,500 = $1 and a gross margin of 70%. The ROI is based on projected profits distributed proportionally to equity ownership.

Assumptions

  • Equity at Launch: Assuming B23 wants to distribute 2,250 shares (30% of 7,500 total shares) at ₦200,000 ($133.33) per share.
  • Pioneer Purchase Equity Conversion:
    • ₦2,000,000 ($1,333.33): 100 shares (1.33% ownership)
    • ₦4,000,000 ($2,666.67): 200 shares (2.67% ownership)
    • ₦5,000,000 ($3,333.33): 250 shares (3.33% ownership)
  • Subscription Plans:
    • Basic: $20/month (₦30,000)
    • Standard: $50/month (₦75,000)
    • Premium: $100/month (₦150,000)
  • MAU Scenarios: 1,000, 5,000, 10,000, and 20,000 MAUs
  • Revenue Distribution: Assumes equal distribution of users across the three subscription plans for simplicity (1/3 each).
  • Gross Margin: 70% (after costs, 70% of revenue is profit).
  • Profit Distribution: Profits are distributed to shareholders proportional to their ownership percentage.
  • Time Frame: ROI is calculated monthly and annually (12 months).
  • Exclusions: Other revenue streams (e.g., vertical upsells, credit systems) are not included to maintain focus on subscription revenue.

Revenue and Profit Calculation

For each MAU scenario, we calculate total revenue, gross profit, and per-share profit:

  • Users per Plan: MAUs ÷ 3 (equal split across Basic, Standard, Premium).
  • Monthly Revenue per Plan:
    • Basic: Users × $20 (₦30,000)
    • Standard: Users × $50 (₦75,000)
    • Premium: Users × $100 (₦150,000)
  • Total Monthly Revenue: Sum of revenue from all plans.
  • Monthly Gross Profit: Total Monthly Revenue × 70%.
  • Annual Gross Profit: Monthly Gross Profit × 12.
  • Per-Share Profit: (Gross Profit ÷ 7,500 shares) × Shareholder’s shares.
  • ROI: (Per-Share Profit ÷ Pioneer Purchase Amount) × 100.

Key Insights

  • Scalability: Higher MAUs significantly increase ROI, with the 20,000 MAU scenario yielding an annual ROI of 46.91% across all pioneer purchase levels.

  • Proportional Returns: ROI percentages are consistent across pioneer purchase amounts because the profit scales linearly with ownership percentage.
  • Growth Potential: At 20,000 MAUs, a ₦5,000,000 ($3,333.33) pioneer purchase could yield $130,366.64 (₦195,549,600) annually, demonstrating significant upside.

Risks and Considerations

  • MAU Uncertainty: Actual user adoption may vary, impacting revenue and ROI.
  • Profit Distribution: Assumes all profits are distributed; actual dividends may depend on company reinvestment strategies.
  • Market Risks: Subscription uptake and retention depend on market acceptance of Project B23’s services.
  • Exclusions: Additional revenue from upsells or credits could increase ROI but is not modeled here.
